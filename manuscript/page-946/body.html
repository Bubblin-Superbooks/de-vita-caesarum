Ix. Upon His First Succeeding To Power, He Felt Such An Abhorrence For The Shedding Of Blood, That, Before His Father’s Arrival In Rome, Calling To Mind The Verse Of Virgil,

Impia quam caesis gens est epulata juvencis,

Ere impious man, restrain’d from blood in vain, Began to feast on flesh of bullocks slain,

he designed to have published a proclamation, “to forbid the sacrifice of oxen.” Before his accession to the imperial authority, and during some time afterwards, he scarcely ever gave the least grounds for being suspected of covetousness or avarice; but, on the contrary, he often afforded proofs, not only of his justice, but his liberality.
