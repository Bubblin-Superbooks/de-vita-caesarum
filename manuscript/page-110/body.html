He Had Made It On The Ides [13Th] Of The Preceding September, At His Lavican Villa, And Committed It To The Custody Of The Chief Of The Vestal Virgins. Quintus Tubero Informs Us, That In All The Wills He Had Signed, From The Time Of His First Consulship To The Breaking Out Of The Civil War, Cneius Pompey Was Appointed His Heir, And That This Had Been Publicly Notified To The Army. But In His Last Will, He Named Three Heirs, The Grandsons Of His Sisters; Namely, Caius Octavius For Three Fourths Of His Estate, And Lucius Pinarius And Quintus Pedius For The Remaining Fourth. Other Heirs [In Remainder] Were Named At The Close Of The Will, In Which He Also Adopted Caius Octavius, Who Was To Assume His Name, Into His Family; And Nominated Most Of Those Who Were Concerned In His Death Among The Guardians Of His Son, If He Should Have Any; As Well As Decimus Brutus Amongst His Heirs Of The Second Order. Be Bequeathed To The Roman People His Gardens Near The Tiber, And Three Hundred Sesterces Each Man.
