Ix. Orbilius Pupillus, Of Beneventum, Being Left An Orphan, By The Death Of His Parents, Who Both Fell A Sacrifice To The Plots Of Their Enemies On The Same Day, Acted, At First, As Apparitor To The Magistrates. He Then Joined The Troops In Macedonia, When He Was First Decorated With The Plumed Helmet , And, Afterwards, Promoted To Serve On Horseback. Having Completed His Military Service, He Resumed His Studies, Which He Had Pursued With No Small Diligence From His Youth Upwards; And, Having Been A Professor For A Long Period In His Own Country, At Last, During The Consulship Of Cicero, Made His Way To Rome, Where He Taught With More Reputation Than Profit. For In One Of His Works He Says, That “He Was Then Very Old, And Lived In A Garret.” He Also Published A Book With The Title Of Perialogos; Containing Complaints Of The Injurious Treatment To Which Professors Submitted, Without Seeking Redress At The Hands Of Parents.
