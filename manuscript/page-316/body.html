Xxxviii.

He was unsparing in the reward of military merit, having granted to above thirty generals the honour of the greater triumph; besides which, he took care to have triamphal decorations voted by the senate for more than that number. That the sons of senators might become early acquainted with the administration of affairs, he permitted them, at the age when they took the garb of manhood , to assume also the distinction of the senatorian robe, with its broad border, and to be present at the debates in the senate-house.
