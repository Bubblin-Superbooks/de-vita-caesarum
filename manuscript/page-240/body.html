Having Thus Given An Account Of The Manner In Which He Filled His Public Offices Both Civil And Military, And His Conduct In The Government Of The Empire, Both In Peace And War; I Shall Now Describe His Private And Domestic Life, His Habits At Home And Among His Friends And Dependents, And The Fortune Attending Him In Those Scenes Of Retirement, From His Youth To The Day Of His Death. He Lost His Mother In His First Consulship, And His Sister Octavia, When He Was In The Fifty-fourth Year Of His Age . He Behaved Towards Them Both With The Utmost Kindness Whilst Living, And After Their Decease Paid The Highest Honours To Their Memory.
