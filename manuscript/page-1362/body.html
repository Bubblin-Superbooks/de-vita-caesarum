Xviii.

He was a great encourager of learning and the liberal arts. He first granted to the Latin and Greek professors of rhetoric the yearly stipend of a hundred thousand sesterces each out of the exchequer. He also bought the freedom of superior poets and artists , and gave a noble gratuity to the restorer of the Coan of Venus , and to another artist who repaired the Colossus .
