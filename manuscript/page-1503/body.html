Crates of Mallos , then, was, in our opinion, the first who introduced the study of grammar at Rome. He was cotemporary with Aristarchus , and having been sent by king Attalus as envoy to the senate in the interval between the second and third Punic wars , soon after the death of Ennius , he had the misfortune to fall into an open sewer in the Palatine quarter of the city, and broke his leg. After which, during the whole period of his embassy and convalescence, he gave frequent lectures, taking much pains to instruct his hearers, and he has left us an example well worthy of imitation.
