Lix. To The Physician Antonius Musa , Who Had Cured Him Of A Dangerous Illness, They Erected A Statue Near That Of Aesculapius, By A General Subscription. Some Heads Of Families Ordered In Their Wills, That Their Heirs Should Lead Victims To The Capitol, With A Tablet Carried Before Them, And Pay Their Vows, “Because Augustus Still Survived.” Some Italian Cities Appointed The Day Upon Which He First Visited Them, To Be Thenceforth The Beginning Of Their Year. And Most Of The Provinces, Besides Erecting Temples And Altars, Instituted Games, To Be Celebrated To His Honour, In Most Towns, Every Five Years.
