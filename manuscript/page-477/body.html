Lv. Besides His Old Friends And Intimate Acquaintance, He Required The Assistance Of Twenty Of The Most Eminent Persons In The City, As Counsellors In The Administration Of Public Affairs. Out Of All This Number, Scarcely Two Or Three Escaped The Fury Of His Savage Disposition. All The Rest He Destroyed Upon One Pretence Or Another; And Among Them Aelius Sejanus, Whose Fall Was Attended With The Ruin Of Many Others. He Had Advanced This Minister To The Highest Pitch Of Grandeur, Not So Much From Any Real Regard For Him, As That By His Base And Sinister Contrivances He Might Ruin The Children Of Germanicus, And Thereby Secure The Succession To His Own Grandson By Drusus.
