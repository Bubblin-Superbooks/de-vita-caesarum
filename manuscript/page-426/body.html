Viii. When He First Applied Himself To Civil Affairs, He Defended The Several Causes Of King Archelaus, The Trallians, And The Thessalians, Before Augustus, Who Sat As Judge At The Trials. He Addressed The Senate On Behalf Of The Laodiceans, The Thyatireans, And Chians, Who Had Suffered Greatly By An Earthquake, And Implored Relief From Rome. He Prosecuted Fannius Caepio, Who Had Been Engaged In A Conspiracy With Varro Muraena Against Augustus, And Procured Sentence Of Condemnation Against Him. Amidst All This, He Had Besides To Superintend Two Departments Of The Administration, That Of Supplying The City With Corn, Which Was Then Very Scarce, And That Of Clearing The Houses Of Correction Throughout Italy, The Masters Of Which Had Fallen Under The Odious Suspicion Of Seizing And Keeping Confined, Not Only Travellers, But Those Whom The Fear Of Being Obliged To Serve In The Army Had Driven To Seek Refuge In Such Places.
