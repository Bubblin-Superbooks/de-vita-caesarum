Xlvi. He Was So Niggardly And Covetous, That He Never Allowed To His Attendants, In His Travels And Expeditions, Any Salary, But Their Diet Only. Once, Indeed, He Treated Them Liberally, At The Instigation Of His Step-father, When, Dividing Them Into Three Classes, According To Their Rank, He Gave The First Six, The Second Four, And The Third Two, Hundred Thousand Sesterces, Which Last Class He Called Not Friends, But Greeks.
